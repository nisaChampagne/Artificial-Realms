❓ Help Guide

Talking to the Dungeon Master
✍
Type freely — the AI DM understands full sentences. "I search the chest" or "I try to bluff the guard" work just as well as numbered options.
🔢
Numbered choices — when the DM gives you options, type 1, 2, or 3 to pick one.
💬
Hint chips — suggested follow-ups appear below the input after each DM response. Click one to send it instantly.
⚡
Quick actions — the row of buttons (Attack, Dodge, Hide…) sends a common action with one click.
How the World Works
📖
The DM tracks the story — you never need to re-explain what happened earlier.
🌍
Your choices shape the world. NPCs remember you, quests branch, and consequences are real.
🎲
The DM will ask you to roll dice at key moments — a prompt appears above the input box.
Quest Tracking
📋
Accepting quests — quests are only logged in your Journal when you accept them, not just when they're offered. Say "I accept" or "Yes, I'll help" to track it.
✅
Quest status — tracked as Active, Completed, or Failed. Quest completion triggers XP rewards and counts toward achievements.
📓
View quests — open your Journal (toolbar or press J) and check the Quests tab to see your current objectives.
Starting Combat
⚡
Initiative — a roll modal appears when combat begins. Your Dexterity modifier is applied automatically. Turn order is tracked in the character sheet.
⚔
Your turn — actions, bonus actions, and reactions reset automatically. Describe your action or use quick buttons.
🎯
Attack rolls — roll d20 + modifiers vs target's AC. A natural 20 is a critical hit (roll damage dice twice).
⏱
Action economy — you get one action, one bonus action, and one reaction per turn. Used actions show as gray pills in your sheet.
Hit Points & Damage
♥
HP to 0 — triggers Death Saving Throws. Roll d20 each turn. Need 10+ to succeed.
✔
3 successes before 3 failures — you stabilize at 0 HP (unconscious but stable).
✖
3 failures before 3 successes — your character dies. A natural 1 counts as 2 failures; a natural 20 instantly restores 1 HP.
🛡
Temporary HP — blue shield displayed above your HP bar. Absorbs damage first before real HP. Doesn't stack (keeps highest value).
🩹
Healing — potions (use from Inventory), spells, or a Rest restore HP. Temp HP can't be healed.
Spellcasting
✨
Spell slots — clickable circles show available slots per level. Casting a spell consumes one slot of that level.
🎯
Spell Save DC — shown in your character sheet stats. Enemies roll vs this DC to resist your spells.
🧠
Concentration — some spells require focus. If you take damage, you roll a CON save to maintain concentration (DC = 10 or half damage, whichever is higher).
🌙
Spell recovery — Warlocks regain slots on short rest. All other casters regain slots on long rest.
Class Resources
⚔
Fighter — Second Wind (self-heal), Action Surge (extra action). Recharge on short/long rest.
💥
Barbarian — Rage (+damage, resistance). Recharge on long rest.
👊
Monk — Ki Points for Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, Step of the Wind. Recharge on short/long rest.
🙏
Cleric — Channel Divinity. Recharge on short/long rest.
✋
Paladin — Lay on Hands (heal pool). Recharge on long rest.
🎵
Bard — Bardic Inspiration (give ally d6–d12 bonus). Recharge on short/long rest.
Conditions & Effects
😵
Conditions — status effects like Poisoned, Stunned, Frightened, Paralyzed appear as colored pills in your sheet.
⏳
Duration — conditions track remaining rounds. They expire automatically on your turn.
🌟
Inspiration — awarded for excellent roleplay. Spend it to gain advantage on any d20 roll (shown as a button in dice prompts). Advantage/disadvantage is otherwise determined by the situation, conditions, and game mechanics.
Resting
⏱
Short Rest (~1 hr) — spend Hit Dice to recover HP. Restores Warlock spell slots and some class resources.
🌟
Long Rest (~8 hrs) — fully restores HP, spell slots, and all class resources. Clears exhaustion levels. Should only be taken in a safe location.
Reading the Map
●
You — your current position on the map.
●
Enemy — a detected threat nearby.
✦
Item — an object, chest, or point of interest.
▬
Door — a passage between rooms or areas.
◆
Landmark — a named location the DM has mentioned (manor, ruins, altar, etc.). Shown with a floating label.
Exploring
🌫
Fog of War — unexplored areas are hidden. Toggle it with the 👁 Fog button on the map panel.
🗺
Scene changes — moving to a new location (dungeon, forest, tavern, manor, ruins, crypt…) generates a fresh map layout.
🎯
As you explore, the map fills in around you. Enemy and item markers are placed by the DM in response to the story.
Toolbar Buttons
🎲
Dice — roll any die (d4–d100) with a modifier. Advantage/disadvantage for ability checks is determined by the game situation, conditions, and DM decisions. You can spend inspiration to gain advantage on any d20 roll.
📜
Character — full character sheet: HP, Ability Scores, Skills, Saving Throws, Features, and Spells.
🌙
Rest — take a Short or Long Rest to recover HP.
📖
Rules — search the official D&D 5e SRD: spells, conditions, equipment, monsters, and more.
👁
Perception — log of all passive and active perception checks made during this session.
📓
Journal — auto-captured NPCs, world lore, key decisions, and quests. Tabs: NPCs · Lore · Decisions · Quests.
🎒
Inventory — manage your equipped and carried items. Tap an item to inspect, equip, use, or drop it.
🔇
DM Voice — toggles text-to-speech narration of the Dungeon Master's responses.
Character Management
📤
Export Character — save your current character as a JSON file. Found in the Character Creation screen. Useful for backups or sharing characters.
📥
Import Character — load a character from a JSON file. Creates a new save slot with the imported character.
🎭
Premade Heroes — 8 ready-to-play characters covering all major classes. Perfect for jumping straight into the adventure.
Saving Your Game
💾
Save (header button) — up to 5 named manual save slots.
⏲
Autosave — your game is saved automatically every 5 minutes to slot 0.
⬆
Auto-updates — when a new version is available, a banner appears at the top. Click to download and install.

🤝

Levelling Up
🔼
When you reach the XP threshold for your next level, a Level Up! notification appears and the DM narrates the moment.
📈
Your Proficiency Bonus increases at levels 5, 9, 13, and 17, boosting all skills and attacks.
⭐
Track your progress in the XP bar in the top header, or open your Character Sheet for the full breakdown.
🏆
The maximum level is 20, requiring 355,000 total XP — the pinnacle of D&D 5e power.

What Are Achievements?
🏆
Milestone tracking — achievements are special milestones that unlock as you progress. Each character has their own achievement progress.
🎉
Unlocking — when you earn an achievement, a toast notification appears with the achievement name and icon.
💾
Per-character — achievements are saved with each character. Starting a new character resets achievement progress for that save.
Combat Achievements
⚔
First Blood — defeat your first enemy in combat.
🐲
Boss Slayer — defeat a boss-level enemy.
💀
Survivor — succeed on a Death Saving Throw.
❤
Iron Will — survive with 3 HP or less.
Progression Achievements
⭐
Rising Star — reach Level 2.
🏆
Champion — reach Level 5 (major power spike).
🌙
Well Rested — take your first Long Rest.
✨
Inspired — spend Inspiration on a roll.
Exploration Achievements
📜
Lore Hunter — discover 5 pieces of world lore (check Journal → Lore tab).
📋
Quest Master — complete 3 quests.
📖
Storyteller — play through 50 narrative turns (back-and-forth exchanges with the DM).
🎒
Pack Rat — carry 10 or more items at once in your inventory.
Tips for Achievement Hunters
🔍
Explore thoroughly — lore is hidden in books, inscriptions, and NPC conversations.
📓
Accept quests when offered — only accepted quests count toward Quest Master.
🎲
Take risks — surviving near-death encounters and boss fights earns unique achievements.
🌙
Don't rush — rest when needed to unlock Well Rested and maintain health for tough fights.
Getting the Most from Your Adventure
🗣
Be specific. "I carefully examine the inscription on the door" gets a richer response than "I look at the door".
🔍
Search everything. Say "I search the room" or "I check for traps" — hidden items and secrets await.
🤝
Talk to NPCs. Most characters have knowledge, quests, or goods. Ask them questions by name.
📓
Check your Journal. NPC names, lore clues, decisions, and quests are auto-captured so you never lose track.
🌍
Change the scene. Say "I travel to the nearest town" or "I enter the dungeon" and the map will update to match.
💡
Use the Rules panel when unsure about a spell, condition, or rule — search right inside the game without alt-tabbing.
💾
Save often. Auto-save runs every 5 minutes, but manual saves protect against unexpected crashes.
🔊
Turn up the volume. The ambient music changes with each scene — dungeon, forest, tavern, manor, and more.
Combat Tips
🎯
Know your modifiers. Check your character sheet for attack bonuses, spell save DC, and skill proficiencies before rolling.
🛡
Armor Class matters. Higher AC enemies require better rolls to hit. Consider using spells with saving throws instead.
✨
Manage spell slots. Don't waste high-level slots on weak enemies — save them for bosses and tough fights.
🧠
Break concentration strategically. If an enemy is under your concentration spell, taking damage might break it. Position wisely.
⚡
Use class features. Second Wind, Rage, Ki Points, and other resources recharge on rests — don't hoard them!
Roleplaying Tips
🎭
Get into character. Describe actions in first person ("I draw my sword and glare at him") for immersive responses.
❓
Ask follow-up questions. "What does the altar look like?" or "Do I recognize this symbol?" deepens the narrative.
🎲
Embrace failure. Failed rolls create memorable stories. A botched persuasion check can lead to unexpected plot twists.
📋
Track your goals. Keep your active quests in mind and mention them to NPCs — they might have relevant information.
